Problem Statement

There are countless numbers of the dog breeds and identifying them can be challenging even to the eye of an expert. However, in certain scenarios knowing the exact breed of a dog can be essential. In this proposed project, we plan to develop a dog breed identification system based on a deep learning model, where the model can identify the breed from an uploaded image from the user. This identification model can be important and useful in several fields and applications; such as in veterinary clinics and dog breeding services.

Dataset

Stanford dogs dataset: It includes 120 breeds of dogs from all over the world. It has been generated based on annotations and images from imageNet to be used for fine grained image classification. This dataset contains 20,580 images, 16,000 of them are used for training and they are provided with class labels and bounding box annotations. The other 4,580 are to be used for testing. The dataset in total is 787 MBs making it very feasible in terms of computational power and training time.
